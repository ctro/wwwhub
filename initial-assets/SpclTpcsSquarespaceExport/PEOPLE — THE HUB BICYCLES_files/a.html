We are group of committed athletes who call the outdoors home, this is essential to the culture of our shop. We pride ourselves on offering the highest quality bicycle service in the Tetons. With our shop in the heart of Jackson Hole we are never far from reach. Every bike brought to the shop is attended to by one of our experienced mechanics. We understand your bike means a lot to you and treat it as if it were your own.

CARY SMITH

Bikes: It's hard for me to pick a favorite bike with so many choices out there. I'd say it depends on my mood. If I want to rip the backyard trails bleeding out of my eyeballs, then the Cannondale FSi is the obvious choice. But if I'm looking to ride downhill irresponsibly fast and get loose, then I grab the Jekyll. For pure, unadulterated fun, the Habit can't be beat. But for almost any race I do, the Scalpel is the fastest tool out there.

Words of Wisdom: One way to keep cycling fresh is to ride with people or in places that will challenge you to improve. The other is to hang out at a bike shop with actual cyclists for employees. Doing both will increase your fitness, skills and list of crude jokes. All of which are important on long rides.

ANDREW WHITEFORD

Bikes:  Ibis Mojo HD3.  It seems every time I get a new bike, it becomes my instant favorite, but this Mojo is a perfect blend of xc adventure bike and full bore crusher for our local trails.  I'm a single bike quiver kind of guy, which allows me to feel completely comfortable on every ride without thinking about having to adapt my riding style to different bikes.

 

Words of Wisdom:  Mix it up!  I definitely have my favorite trails and riding style, but I love studying maps and finding new areas to explore.  I dig the challenge of riding less frequented trails that weren't built to our present standards because it forces me to really focus on the terrain ahead and adjust to whatever comes my way.  It becomes a really fun compliment to riding the trails I know by heart, where I'm able to have a greater focus on speed, technique, or style.  I also recommend going for a hike every once in a while and doing some trail maintenance- which I usually do on a rainy day- so that I can study the trails in a more intimate manner.  After those sessions, I always ride the trail better and carry a greater appreciation for our awesome trail network and all the work that has gone into them.
